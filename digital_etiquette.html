What is Digital Etiquette?

Digital etiquette (or "netiquette") refers to the responsible and respectful way of communicating and behaving when using digital technology, including social media, email, messaging apps, and online forums.

üåü Key Principles of Digital Etiquette

1. Think Before You Post

Why it matters: Once something is online, it can be there forever. Screenshots can be taken, and content can be shared beyond your intended audience.

Best practice: Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my teacher, parent, or future employer saw this?"

2. Respect Others' Privacy

Why it matters: Not everyone wants their photos, location, or personal information shared online.

Best practice: Always ask permission before posting photos of others or sharing their personal information.

3. Use Appropriate Language

Why it matters: Offensive language can hurt others and damage your reputation.

Best practice: Avoid profanity, hate speech, and discriminatory language. Write as if you're speaking to someone face-to-face.

4. Be Mindful of Tone

Why it matters: Text doesn't convey tone well, and messages can be misunderstood.

Best practice: Use emojis appropriately to convey tone. Avoid ALL CAPS (it seems like shouting). Read your message before sending.

5. Respond Appropriately to Conflict

Why it matters: Online conflicts can escalate quickly and cause lasting damage to relationships.

Best practice: Don't engage with trolls. Report harassment. Take difficult conversations offline when possible.

6. Respect Intellectual Property

Why it matters: Using others' work without permission is theft and can have legal consequences.

Best practice: Give credit when sharing others' content. Don't copy homework or claim others' work as your own.

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Digital Etiquette Mistakes to Avoid

  • Oversharing: Posting too much personal information or every detail of your life
  • Cyberbullying: Using technology to harass, threaten, or embarrass others
  • Ghosting: Suddenly stopping all communication without explanation
  • Public arguments: Having heated debates or personal conflicts in public forums
  • Spam posting: Flooding feeds with repetitive or irrelevant content
  • Inappropriate tagging: Tagging people in posts they wouldn't want to be associated with

‚úÖ Platform-Specific Etiquette Tips

WhatsApp & Messaging Apps

  • Don't add people to groups without asking
  • Respect "Do Not Disturb" hours
  • Use voice notes considerately (not everyone can listen immediately)
  • Don't forward unverified information or fake news

Social Media (Facebook, Instagram, TikTok)

  • Think twice before posting when emotional
  • Use privacy settings appropriately
  • Don't post photos of others without permission
  • Be authentic - don't create fake personas

Email & Professional Communication

  • Use clear, descriptive subject lines
  • Be concise but polite
  • Use proper greetings and sign-offs
  • Proofread before sending

üõ°Ô∏è When Digital Etiquette Goes Wrong

If you experience harassment or cyberbullying:

  • Don't respond to the harasser
  • Document the behavior (screenshots)
  • Block and report the person
  • Tell a trusted adult
  • Know that it's not your fault

If you make a mistake:

  • Acknowledge it quickly
  • Apologize sincerely
  • Delete inappropriate content
  • Learn from the experience
  • Make amends if necessary
