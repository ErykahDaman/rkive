üìö Learn About Cybersecurity & Data Privacy

What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting your devices, networks, and data from digital attacks, theft, and damage. In today's connected world, cybersecurity knowledge is essential for everyone.

üõ°Ô∏è Core Cybersecurity Concepts

1. Password Security

Why it matters: Weak passwords are the #1 way hackers gain access to accounts. Over 80% of data breaches involve weak or stolen passwords.

Best practices:

  • Length: Use at least 12 characters (longer is better)
  • Complexity: Mix uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
  • Uniqueness: Never reuse passwords across accounts
  • Avoid personal info: Don't use names, birthdays, or common words
  • Example of strong password: M0rningCoffee@7AM! (but don't use this one!)

2. Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

What it is: An extra security layer that requires two forms of verification to access your account.

How it works: Something you know (password) + something you have (phone/app)

Types of 2FA:

  • SMS codes: Text messages with temporary codes
  • Authenticator apps: Google Authenticator, Authy (more secure)
  • Hardware keys: Physical devices like YubiKey (most secure)
  • Biometrics: Fingerprint or face recognition

3. Phishing Attacks

What it is: Fraudulent attempts to steal your personal information through fake emails, websites, or messages.

Common phishing tactics:

  • Fake bank emails: "Your account is suspended, click here"
  • Prize scams: "You've won! Claim your prize now"
  • Urgent requests: "Update your password immediately"
  • Fake social media: Friend requests from unknown people

How to spot phishing:

  • Check sender's email address carefully
  • Look for spelling and grammar errors
  • Hover over links to see real destinations
  • Be suspicious of urgent or threatening language
  • Verify requests through official channels

4. Malware Protection

Types of malware:

  • Viruses: Attach to files and spread when shared
  • Ransomware: Encrypts your files and demands payment
  • Spyware: Secretly monitors your activities
  • Adware: Shows unwanted advertisements
  • Trojans: Disguised as legitimate software

Protection strategies:

  • Keep operating system and software updated
  • Use reputable antivirus software
  • Don't download software from untrusted sources
  • Be cautious with email attachments
  • Regular backups of important data

üîê Data Privacy Fundamentals

1. Personal Data Protection

What is personal data: Any information that can identify you - name, ID number, phone, email, location, photos, browsing history.

Privacy risks:

  • Identity theft: Using your info to impersonate you
  • Financial fraud: Unauthorized access to bank accounts
  • Social engineering: Using personal info to manipulate you
  • Data broker sales: Companies selling your information

2. Social Media Privacy

Privacy settings checklist:

  • Set profile to private/friends only
  • Turn off location sharing
  • Disable facial recognition
  • Review tagged photos before they appear
  • Limit who can search for you
  • Turn off ad tracking

Smart sharing practices:

  • Don't post your full birthdate
  • Avoid sharing travel plans in real-time
  • Don't post photos of important documents
  • Be careful about check-ins and location tags
  • Think before posting anything permanent

3. Mobile Device Security

Essential mobile security:

  • Screen lock: Use PIN, pattern, fingerprint, or face unlock
  • App permissions: Only grant necessary permissions
  • Official app stores: Download only from Google Play/App Store
  • Wi-Fi security: Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities
  • Remote wipe: Enable find-my-device features

4. VPN (Virtual Private Network)

What it does: Creates a secure, encrypted connection between your device and the internet.

Benefits:

  • Hides your IP address and location
  • Encrypts your internet traffic
  • Protects on public Wi-Fi
  • Can bypass geographical restrictions
  • Prevents ISP tracking

When to use VPN: Public Wi-Fi, sensitive transactions, privacy protection, accessing blocked content.

üö® African-Specific Cyber Threats

Mobile Money Fraud

  • SIM swap attacks: Criminals steal your phone number
  • Fake mobile money agents: Unauthorized payment points
  • PIN harvesting: Tricks to get your mobile money PIN
  • Protection: Never share PINs, verify agents, use official channels

WhatsApp Scams

  • Friend impersonation: "Help me, I'm in trouble"
  • Fake job offers: "Earn money from home"
  • Investment schemes: "Double your money"
  • Protection: Verify through voice calls, be skeptical of urgent requests

‚úÖ Daily Cybersecurity Habits

  • Morning: Check for overnight alerts or suspicious activities
  • Before clicking: Verify sender and link destinations
  • When connecting: Ensure Wi-Fi networks are secure
  • Before downloading: Check app permissions and reviews
  • Evening: Log out of sensitive accounts
  • Weekly: Review account activities and privacy settings
  • Monthly: Update passwords and software
